## A second sample for route-based writing

This starter entry shows how a trip page can combine place, movement, and mood without becoming a full travel guide.

Think of each paragraph as a stop on the route map. The goal is not to explain everything, but to preserve the emotional geometry of the trip: where it opened, where it slowed down, and where it left a mark.

## A good rhythm for these pages

- Open with a strong first image from the city.
- Let the route map and the text echo each other.
- Keep captions and notes short enough that the page still breathes.
